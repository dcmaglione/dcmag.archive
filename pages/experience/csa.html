COMPUTER SCIENCE AMBASSADORS

As Computer Science Ambassadors, our mission is to serve as a platform for a more collaborative, inclusive, and unified computer science community. We achieve this by encouraging and fostering a diverse community of students through peer mentorship, community-building events, and support from the computer science student-led organizations. Ultimately, we want to foster and encourage a diverse community, serving as a social network to qualify the BU CS experience as a whole.


Ambassadors should strive to achieve the following goals to maintain an inclusive and innovative CS environment.

  • Bridge the between Boston University's Computer Science programs and the students that benefit from them via active communication.
  • Become a reliable resource for computer science students regarding classes, clubs, internships, and other resources.
  • Plan and attend semester events to foster a diverse and engaging community.
  • Act as a mentor to a small group of incoming CS freshmen.

ENGAGEMENT COMMITTEE

I currently serve on the Engagement Committee for the Computer Science Ambassadors. Ambassadors are required to volunteer for at least one committee per term. Committees are up to the Executive Board to create, delegate, and lead.


As a member of the Engagement Committee, I am responsible for working on attendance, point checking, forms, and overall compliance. This includes tracking the responsibilities of fellow ambassadors, offices, and other committees. Additionally, our goal is to boost internal and external involvement in the CSA program while maintaining close relations with BU faculty.
